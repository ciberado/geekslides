CSS slide layout catalog

15 popular slide layouts, each achievable with CSS. Diagrams show the structural composition of each layout.

Title slide

Centered title and subtitle on a full-bleed background. The simplest opener.

Title + content

Heading at the top, body area below for bullets, text, or a simple list.

Two-column text

Content split into two equal columns for comparisons or dense material.

Image + text (half and half)

Left half is a full-height image or color block; right half holds the headline and copy.

Full-bleed image + overlay

Image fills the entire slide; a semi-transparent overlay anchors the headline at top or bottom.

Section header / divider

Large centered label that marks the start of a new chapter in the deck.

Three-column cards

Three equal-width cards in a row, each with an icon or stat, label, and short description.

Big stat / pull quote

A single oversized number or short quote dominates the slide, with a small label beneath.

Timeline / process steps

Horizontal steps connected by a line or arrows, each with a number, label, and short description.

Chart / data slide

Title and subtitle at top, chart or graph takes up most of the content area, with a legend below.

Comparison (A vs B)

Two panels side by side divided by a center line, each with its own heading and attributes.

Team / people grid

Headshots or avatar circles in a row with a name and role below each one.

Image grid / mood board

A grid of equally-sized image placeholders fills the slide, with a title bar at the top.

Table / matrix

A structured table with a header row, used for feature comparisons, schedules, or data grids.

Blank / canvas

Empty slide with a subtle border or background, used for custom diagrams, freehand, or whiteboard content.
